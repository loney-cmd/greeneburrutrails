The Western Summit Trail

Scenery and Features

EXTENSIVE tracts of unspoiled primary forest, replete with magnificent old trees, are a feature of Mau Eburu’s scenic Western Summit Trail. This exhilarating Nature Trail offers stunning views as well – of **Lake Nakuru** to the north, and of **Lake Naivasha** to the south-east.

Other attractive features include **’46 Dam (Lengina)** – an old weir built to capture water from a natural spring at the head of the Saimo Valley. The dam is a tranquil spot fringed by tall *Dombeya* trees.

Habitats and Wildlife

Around this summit, there is an extensive belt of **African Mountain Bamboo**. The undisturbed forest habitats support a rich and diverse wild fauna. **Bushbuck** and **Harvey’s Red Duikers** are often glimpsed, and sounders of **Giant Forest Hogs** may be encountered.

Signs of **African Buffaloes**, and of prowling **Leopards**, may also be found. The abundant birdlife on view may include those stunning forest species, **Hartlaub’s Turaco and the Bar-tailed Trogon**.

Route Notes and Access ⚠️

The hike is **$6.4\text{ km}$** starting from the Central Forest Glade Campsite and ending above Morop Gate. The Trail begins and ends at different points, so hiking parties wishing to complete the whole Trail may have to be **dropped off at Forest Glade and then collected later from the Morop parking area**.

Alternatively, hiking parties can retrace their steps from Lengina Dam, making a return hike of **$10.8\text{ km}$** to Forest Glade. The Trail is overgrown in places, and paths may not be obvious. **So it is best to hire a local guide.**
