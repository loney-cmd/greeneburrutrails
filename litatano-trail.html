Trail Overview and Habitats

The Litatano Spring Trail is a beautiful 6 km hike that showcases a striking variety of **contrasting natural habitats**. Starting from the **Kahuho Gate**, the route traverses open **Acacia woodland**, passes through a wide belt of aromatic **Leleshwa Bush** (*Tarchonanthus camphoratus*), and finally ascends into tall stands of mixed **broad-leaf upland forest** and open-canopy **African Pencil Cedar** (*Juniperus procera*) forest.

As you ascend through the silvery-grey Leleshwa bushes, you'll be treated to a camphor-like scent and a view dominated by the imposing **Mount Eburu Western Summit**, often shrouded in swirling clouds.

This variety of habitats supports an equally varied wild fauna, making the trail an exceptional spot for **birdwatchers**:

  • **Conspicuous Birds:** Look for dazzling **Golden-winged Sunbirds** (of several species) feeding on flowering shrubs.

The Destination: Litatano Spring

The trail follows a broad ridge before descending into the lush green Kahuho Valley. The final destination is the **Litatano Spring**, a small spring issuing from the base of a secluded valley cliff.

  • **Altitude:** The spring is found at an altitude of **$2,479\text{ m}$**.
  • **Picnic Spot:** The small pool formed by the spring is a **delightfully peaceful and tranquil spot**, making it an **ideal base for a picnic** or for short sightseeing forays into the upper valley.

Important Safety and Access Notes ‚ö†Ô∏è

Be aware that the ground-cover on the moist valley floor is dense and **rife with clumps of chest-high Stinging Nettles**.

Visitors are strongly advised to wear **protective clothing**, specifically **long trousers over long socks, and long-sleeved shirts or jackets**, to avoid impediment from nettles.

Approximately $270\text{ m}$ beyond the Kahuho Gate, you will find the attractive **Kichwa Nyati (Buffalo Head) Campsite**.
