The Ngalumoni Rock Shelter

The trail leads to the **Ngalumoni Rock Shelter**, a secluded cave set back in the side-wall of a deep valley. The shelter, concealed by tall tree tops, has a blackened ceiling from generations of cooking fires and was historically used by the local **Ogiek (Il Torobo) community** for gathering honey.

The shelter is up to five metres deep and offers excellent refuge from rain and wind.

Scenery and Forest Life

This hike offers **spectacular mountain scenery**. A key viewpoint ($1.8\text{ km}$ in) commands a spectacular vista down a rugged, steep-sided valley, with **Lake Naivasha** visible in the distance.

The surrounding habitats feature impressive stands of **African Pencil Cedar** (*Juniperus procera*) and **Podo** (*Podocarpus milanjianus*) trees. Look for foraging troops of **Guereza Colobus Monkeys** and **Blue Monkeys**. Signs of Buffaloes and even **Mountain Bongos** are occasionally found.

Important Safety and Access Notes ⚠️

The Trail starts near the **Morop Gate**. The final $200\text{ m}$ descent to the rock shelter is **very steep** and must be negotiated carefully using available hand- and footholds among tree roots.

The trail can be challenging to follow; **the hire of a local guide is strongly recommended.**
