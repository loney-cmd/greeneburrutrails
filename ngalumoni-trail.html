The Ngalumoni Rock Shelter

The **Ngalumoni Rock Shelter**, set back from a narrow shelf of rock in the side-wall of a deep valley, is the trail's namesake. The shelter is very well concealed, not visible until you are on the ledge beside its mouth.

The shelter has long been used by members of the **Ogiek (Il Torobo) community** as a base to gather honey. Smoke from generations of fires has blackened its roof and walls.

Scenery and Forest Life

The Trail offers a hike amid **spectacular mountain scenery**. One viewpoint, $1.8\text{ km}$ from the start, commands a spectacular vista down a rugged valley, with **Lake Naivasha in the distance**.

The forest features impressive stands of **African Pencil Cedar** (*Juniperus procera*) and **Podo** (*Podocarpus milanjianus*) trees. Look out over the closed forest canopy for foraging troops of **Guereza (Black-and-White) Colobus Monkeys** and **Blue Monkeys**.

Route Notes and Access ⚠️

The Trail begins at a small parking area, $1.4\text{ km}$ up the steep ’46 Dam (Lengina) Forest Track from Morop Gate. The final $200\text{-m}$ descent to the rock-shelter has to be negotiated carefully, **using available hand- and footholds** among the roots of trees that are wedged into the rock.

As with the other trails, **the hire of a local guide is strongly recommended.**
