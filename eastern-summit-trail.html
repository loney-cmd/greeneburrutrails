The Western Summit Trail

Quick Facts ⏱️

DetailValue
Difficulty:Moderate to Difficult
Duration:4–5 hours (Leisurely Hike)
Distance:6.4 km (One-Way Circuit) / 10.8 km (Return Option)
Highest Point:2,823 m
Starting Point:Central Forest Glade Campsite

Trail Highlights and Scenery

This trail features **extensive tracts of unspoiled primary forest** with magnificent old trees. It offers stunning views of **Lake Nakuru** to the north and **Lake Naivasha** to the south-east over the closed canopy.

Key feature is the **’46 (Lengina) Dam**, an old weir built in colonial times to capture spring water in the Saimo Valley. This tranquil spot is visited by wildlife and is below a spectacular, sheer rock-face.

Wildlife and Forest Species

The undisturbed habitats support a rich and diverse fauna. **Bushbuck** and **Harvey’s Red Duikers** are often glimpsed. Signs of African Buffaloes and even prowling **Leopards** may be found.

The abundant birdlife includes stunning forest species like **Hartlaub’s Turaco** and the **Bar-tailed Trogon**.

The summit area, known locally as *Emoguo Ereko* (‘Hill of Red Soil’), contains an extensive belt of **African Mountain Bamboo**.

Important Safety and Access Notes ⚠️

The trail is a one-way hike (Forest Glade to Morop) and requires **vehicle drop-off/collection** at the Morop parking area. Alternatively, you can climb back over the Western Summit Ridge, making a $10.8\text{ km}$ return hike to Forest Glade.

The trail is overgrown in places; **hiring a local guide is strongly recommended.**
