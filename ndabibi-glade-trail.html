The Ndabibi–Forest Glade Trail

Quick Facts ⏱️

DetailValue
Difficulty:Moderate
Duration:5–6 hours
Distance:10.7 km Circuit
Highest Point:2,538 m
Starting Point:Gathondia Road-head (near Ndabibi Gate)

Features and Scenery

This $10.7\text{ km}$ circuit presents a unique opportunity to explore vast areas of the **closed-canopy forest**. The trail offers scenic views over the forest canopy toward **Eastern Mau** and across the **Lake Naivasha Basin**.

The highest point is in closed-canopy forest at $2,538\text{ m}$. **Forest Glade**, $5.2\text{ km}$ from the road-head, is an open, convenient site for picnics and a starting point for other major trails.

Wildlife and Forest Sounds

Hikers may see Bushbuck, Harvey’s Red Duikers, and Giant Forest Hogs. **Blue Monkeys** may be seen in the canopy. Look for an active **African Crowned Eagles’ nest** high up in a forest tree.

The forest's sounds are a major highlight: listening for the plaintive refrain of a **Yellow-whiskered Greenbul** or the resonant, ascending calls of **Hartlaub’s Turacos** adds a thrilling awareness of the forest’s life.

Important Access Notes ⚠️

Access to the Gathondia road-head is via a track in **very poor condition**. Vehicles may need to be left at the Trading Centre, and **secure parking should be arranged by your local guide**.

Due to the distance, an **early start** is recommended to allow ample time to explore the upper reaches of the trail.
