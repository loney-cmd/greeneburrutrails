The Ndabibi–Forest Glade Trail

Quick Facts ⏱️

DetailValue
Distance:10.7 km Circuit
Duration:5–6 hours (Recommended Outing)
Highest Point:2,538 m
Starting Point:Gathondia road-head (Near Ndabibi Gate)

Overview and Features

The Ndabibi–Forest Glade Circuit is a **$10.7\text{-km}$ circuit** presenting the opportunity to explore parts of the **closed-canopy forest** lining the upper sections of the Trail. Scenic views over the forest canopy towards **Eastern Mau** and out across the **Lake Naivasha Basin** are another of this Trail’s appealing features.

The Trail’s highest point is $2,538\text{ m}$ above sea level. **Forest Glade**, $5.2\text{ km}$ from the road-head, is a convenient, open site for picnics and a designated Campsite.

Wildlife and Birding

In the forest, you may get to see **Bushbuck** and **Harvey’s Red Duikers**. **Giant Forest Hogs** have been encountered, and **Blue Monkeys** may be seen up in the canopy. There is an active **African Crowned Eagles’ nest** beside the Trail.

Birding in the mature forest around Forest Glade can be hugely rewarding. Here, it is what you hear, as much as what you see, which imparts a thrilling awareness of the forest’s abundant life (e.g., **Yellow-whiskered Greenbul** and **Hartlaub’s Turacos**).

Access Notes ⚠️

The circuit starts from and returns to the Gathondia road-head, about $900\text{ m}$ outside the Ndabibi Gate. The track leading up to the road-head is in **very poor condition**. Vehicles may have to be left at the Gathondia Trading Centre. **Secure parking can be arranged by your local guide.**

An **early start** will allow ample time to explore the Trail’s scenic upper reaches.
