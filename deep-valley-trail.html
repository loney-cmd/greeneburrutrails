The Deep Valley (Waterfall) Trail

Quick Facts ⏱️

DetailValue
Principal Attraction:Spectacular Waterfall
Distance:About 4.6 km (Round Trip)
Duration:4–5 hours (Required due to terrain)
Starting Point:Central Forest Glade Campsite

Overview and Scenery

The Ndabibi River Valley, known as Deep Valley, harbours some of the **most rugged and dramatic scenery** to be found within the Mau Eburu Forest. A hike in this secluded valley, although demanding physically, makes for an **exhilarating adventure experience**.

Spectacular forest-covered cliffs and bare walls of sheer, exposed rock line both sides of the valley. In places, it is so narrow and deep that only a thin sliver of sky can be seen overhead. This stupendous chasm is the result of aeons of gouging action by the headwaters of the **Ndabibi River**, one of Mau Eburu’s most important water catchments.

The Waterfall and Wildlife

A walk of about $300\text{ m}$ upstream, between the flanks of the deep, narrowing chasm, leads to the **Deep Valley Waterfall**. The cascading water, tumbling from a rock-shelf high above, falls ten metres or so into a small pool, raising a fine spray.

The river here is a clear, shallow stream, flowing around moss-covered boulders and shaded by luxuriant **tree-ferns**. Around the pool beneath the waterfall, the spoor of **Mountain Bongos** and of **Giant Forest Hogs** is very often evident.

Challenge and Safety ⚠️

The terrain is formidable. The ascent/descent is **very steep and challenging**, calling for careful use of available hand- and footholds. In some places, the ground-cover is so dense, there is no obvious route.

The services of an **experienced leader or guide are essential**. **Waterproof hiking boots are also recommended** due to the sodden turf.
