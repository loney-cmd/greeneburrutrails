The Deep Valley (Waterfall) Trail

A True Wilderness Adventure

The Deep Valley Trail explores the narrow and precipitous upper reaches of the dramatic **Ndabibi River Valley**. This area harbors some of the most rugged and dramatic scenery in Mau Eburu, offering the uniquely satisfying appeal of a genuine, full-blooded wilderness adventure.

The **spectacular chasm** is the result of aeons of gouging action by the headwaters of the Ndabibi River. In places, the valley is so deep and narrow that only a thin sliver of sky is visible overhead.

The Waterfall

The trail’s principal attraction is a **spectacular waterfall**, cascading over a sheer rock-face at the head of the valley. The water tumbles ten metres into a small pool, where the valley’s two flanks almost meet.

On the valley floor, the Ndabibi River is a clear, shallow stream flowing around moss-covered boulders, shaded by luxuriant **tree-ferns**.

Extreme Difficulty and Safety ⚠️

This trail is **physically demanding**. The descent into the valley is very steep and challenging, calling for careful use of available hand- and footholds.

In some places, the ground-cover is extremely dense with no obvious route. **The services of an experienced leader or guide are essential.**

Waterproof hiking boots are highly recommended due to the slippery, sodden turf near the river.

Spoor of **Mountain Bongos** and **Giant Forest Hogs** is often evident around the waterfall pool.
